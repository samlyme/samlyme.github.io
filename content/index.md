# Hi! Welcome to my site!

I am **Sam Ly**[^1]. 

I am a Computer Science student taking a minor in data science and a minor in mathematics (potentially a double major if time allows). I am interested in all things programming and math! Most of all, I am a **continual learner** and **creator**. I will never turn down an opportunity to learn something new, solve an interesting problem, or make something cool.

My philosophy on learning is this: **learn just enough to build fast**. That is, I take my time learn something deeply (but not too much!), then **iterate fast** on janky prototypes until it clicks. This is where I either go back to the books, or continue jank-ing away until I have something polished. Books lay the foundation for true understanding, but can only take you so far. The only way to truly understand is to create, and all creation requires iteration. 

There is almost a phase transition when *concepts* become *creations*. These abstract *things* swirling in my head like suddenly become real, tangible. Something that I can measure with benchmarks, see with my own eyes, or feel the weight of in my hand. 

Whether it be a difficult math proof, programming problem, or a paragraph I've been struggling to word, the moment when things finally click is a feeling I am always chasing. 

Currently, I am a **research assistant** working under [Dr. Hao Ji](https://vccjihao.github.io/index.html) in the [Computational Intelligence](https://www.cpp.edu/sci/computer-science/student-labs/research-labs.shtml) lab. We tackle the problem of applying machine learning to create efficient sparse linear solvers.
